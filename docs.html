What can you do?

These are some advices to help protect yourself on the internet!

  1. Do NOT share your password to ANYONE.
  2. Do NOT use the same password, security questions and answers for multiple important accounts.
  3. Use a password that has at least 20 characters, use at least one number, one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter and one special symbol.
  4. Do NOT use the names of your families, friends or pets in your passwords.
  5. Do NOT use postcodes, house numbers, phone numbers, birthdates, ID card numbers, social security numbers, and so on in your passwords
  6. Do NOT click on any suspicious email or SMS message.
  7. Lock your computer and mobile phone when you leave them.
  8. It's recommended to change your passwords every 10 weeks.
  9. Turn on 2-step authentication whenever possible.
  10. When travelling, you can encrypt your Internet connections before they leave your laptop, tablet, mobile phone or router. For example, you can set up a private VPN with protocols like WireGuard( or IKEv2, OpenVPN, SSTP, L2TP over IPSec ) on your own server( home computer, dedicated server or VPS ) and connect to it. Alternatively, you can set up an encrypted SSH tunnel between your computer and your own server and configure Chrome or FireFox to use socks proxy. Then even if somebody captures your data as it is transmitted between your device( e.g. laptop, iPhone, iPad ) and your server with a packet sniffer, they'll won't be able to steal your data and passwords from the encrypted streaming data.
  11. Do NOT send sensitive information online via unencrypted (e.g.HTTP or FTP) connections. You should use encrypted connections such as HTTPS, SFTP, FTPS, SMTPS, IPSec whenever possible.
